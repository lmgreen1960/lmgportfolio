RecipeBox

Store & share your favorite family recipes.
Search & save your new favs.

  • Date: January 2020

The problem

Whether you are a gourmet chef, a home cook or a weekend baker you have spent time and effort collecting recipes. You have family heritage recipes, "secret" recipes and recipes that you have tweaked and perfected. You've saved them in binders, on cards, in boxes, digitally and even in your head. Sometimes you lose them and you're often frustrated when you can't find that one recipe that you know is exactly what you want. Can a website/app be created that allows users to safely store prized family recipes as well as new favorites in a single place and easily retrieve, use and share their recipes?

The process

01 Research

Cooks and bakers are passionate about their recipes! Once they find a recipe that works for them, they want to return to it time and again and they may want to share it or keep it private. Our users cook 2-4 times a week and would LOVE to have a single place to store their recipes.

They are frustrated having to wade through blogs just to get to the list of ingredients and they want their favorite family recipes at their fingertips. An analysis of similar websites and apps revealed that competitor sites focus on meal planning, cooking lessons or their own content rather than the content of the user.


02 Ideation and Features

The MoSCoW method and "I Like, I Wish, What If?" were useful in prioritizing the features. These three key features filtered to the top as the most important:

  • Storage - Users need to enter recipes easily with the option of adding photos.
  • Searching - Users need to easily find their own recipes and “favorite” new recipes.
  • Sharing - Users want to share their favorite recipes with family and friends.


03Brand and UI

The key elements of our brand and user interface design:

  • Original logo designed by Carissa Moses
  • Warm, muted, vintage color palette representing food colors of wine, cream and light mandarin
  • Open, readable, san serif font of Quicksand
  • Generally simple icons and buttons so as not to complete with user content

We stayed true to our approachable, warm and friendly brand, finding simple icons and fonts that would be readable for users and visually appealing while not competing with user content. Our team co-created a vintage color palette that started with muted food colors. We agreed that our brand would lean away from traditional food colors such as bright reds and yellow. We used only three of our colors, a few buttons and icons in our final design to keep things clean.


04 Test and Refine

User tests yielded these findings:

  • users want the option to include their own photos
  • users want to add custom tags for organization that makes sense to them
  • users had trouble finding the profile icon
  • users did not know the difference between saving and favoriting recipes with the heart icon
  • users want to know how many recipes are in each of their custom catergories
  • the grid of recipes should have the same spacing for rows and columns and the captions are too close to the photo
  • menu font is too small and light

    • We iterated all of the above changes into our final clickable prototype.


The conclusion

Our goal was to provide home cooks and bakers with a reliable way to store, search and share recipes. RecipeBox provides not only a convenient location but a way to personally customize and search content that makes sense to the user!
